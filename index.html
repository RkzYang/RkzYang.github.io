Yang’s Strategy Blog

Welcome to my blog! I am a Ph.D. candidate in {Strategy} @ the University of California, Riverside (my current C.V.). I like to think about strategy and program in R & Python.

My research concerns firm agentic capabilities to impact and react to the market environments (a.k.a., the interplay between environment-shaping strategies and performance-shaping environment). Currently, I study how mergers and acquisitions (M&As) change a firm's position in relation to its peers and the structure of inter-firm network, which drives firm market valuations.

As a data science hobbyist and a management theorist, I realize two things: one, data allows me to see more and think deeper; two, human brains are built to produce and consume theories. As we envision and observe how things connect, our minds naturally turn to what makes them tick. With me, knowledge production/application is a loop of causal inference and probabilistic prediction.
